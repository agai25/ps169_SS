HOW THE GOVERNMENT WORKS

INTRODUCTION

The United States government is like a team where different people have different jobs. Our government has three main parts, called "branches." Each branch has its own special job to do. This system was created by the Constitution, which is like a rulebook for our country.

THE THREE BRANCHES OF GOVERNMENT

THE LEGISLATIVE BRANCH: THE LAWMAKERS

The Legislative Branch is like the rule-makers of our country. They meet in a building called the Capitol in Washington, D.C.

This branch includes:

  • The Senate (100 members - 2 from each state)
  • The House of Representatives (435 members - more from states with more people)

Together, they are called Congress. Their main job is to make laws for our country. They also decide how to spend the country's money and can check the work of the President.

THE EXECUTIVE BRANCH: THE RULE ENFORCERS

The Executive Branch makes sure people follow the laws. The President is the leader of this branch.

This branch includes:

  • The President
  • The Vice President
  • Cabinet members who help the President

The President's job is to lead the country and the military. The President also works with leaders from other countries. When Congress makes a law, the President can sign it to make it official or veto it if they think it's not a good idea.

THE JUDICIAL BRANCH: THE JUDGES

The Judicial Branch decides if laws follow the rules in the Constitution. The Supreme Court is the highest court in our country.

This branch includes:

  • The Supreme Court (9 judges called Justices)
  • Other federal courts across the country

These judges help solve disagreements about laws. They make sure that laws are fair and follow the Constitution.

WORKING TOGETHER: CHECKS AND BALANCES

Each branch has the power to check the other branches to make sure no one gets too powerful. This is called "checks and balances." Here are some examples:

  • The President can veto (say no to) laws from Congress
  • Congress can override the President's veto if enough members vote for it
  • The Supreme Court can decide if laws are allowed by the Constitution
  • Congress must approve the President's choices for important jobs

This system helps make sure our government is fair and that everyone follows the rules in the Constitution.

VOCABULARY

Constitution: The set of basic rules and principles that describes how our government works.

Branch: One of the three main parts of the government, each with different powers and responsibilities.

Congress: The legislative branch of the U.S. government, made up of the Senate and House of Representatives.

Veto: When the President rejects a law passed by Congress.

Checks and balances: A system that gives each branch of government the power to limit the actions of the other branches.
